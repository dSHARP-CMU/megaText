We've designated the data from this .csv file, as ``df'' to denote our
primary data frame.

Our data frame contains 14 different series, and drawn together from a
combination of sources including imdb.com, and Vincent Terrace's
\emph{Encyclopedia of Television}.

In the data frame, these series are displayed as the following columns:

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist

  matched otherwise displays imdb title)
\item
  \textbf{program\_type} - (from Terrace)

\item
  \textbf{network} - (from Terrace)
\item
  \textbf{program\_description} - (from Terrace)
\item
  \textbf{first\_air\_year} - (from Terrace)
\item
  \textbf{last\_air\_year} - (from Terrace)
\item
  \textbf{genre(3)} - (from imdb; the number denotes the info\_type\_id

  \textbf{plot(98)} - (from imdb)

  \textbf{trivia(17)} - (from imdb)
\item
